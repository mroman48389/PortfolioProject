About me

Professional experience

Philosophies

  • KISS

    Simpler is generally better. Prefer readability over short, "clever" code. Fewer bugs. Fewer headaches. Fewer late-night debugging sessions. Faster development and better maintainability. Be kind to your future self and others.

  • Team health matters

    A strong, diverse team is a force to be reckoned with. Support, encourage, learn from, and teach your teammates. Bring out the best in one another. If criticism isn't constructive, keep it to yourself. Negativity is a cancer to teamwork and organizations. Code that is maintainable, readable, consistent, organized, efficient, and well-documented will preserve the team's sanity.

  • UI/UX is king

    An app can have flawless functionality, but it won't mean much if it doesn't look pretty and make the user want to use it. UI/UX should be user-friendly and intuitive.

  • Always ask why

    It's critical to know why code works like it does, not just what it does. Code is a tool, and all tools are only good if used properly. The larger an app becomes, the more important it is to build it on a solid foundation of well-designed code. Never stop digging into the "whys" behind what you're building. Unless you're into clunky systems teeming with tricky bugs.

  • No surrender

    Above all, never give up. There is a logical reason behind every bug and often multiple ways of solving problems. Software engineering requires persistence.
